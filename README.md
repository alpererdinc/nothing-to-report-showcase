# Nothing To Report

**Nothing To Report** is a 2D cyber-noir newsroom game prototype where the player works behind the desk of an independent media channel in a corrupted future city.

The city says there is nothing to report.
The documents disagree.

---

## Overview

**Nothing To Report** places the player in the role of an editor during a night shift at a small independent newsroom.

The player investigates official reports, field notes, camera logs, broken voice messages, visual evidence, and backchannel signal packets to uncover contradictions behind each case.

The main question is not only:

> What is true?

but also:

> How much of the truth can we publish without getting someone hurt?

The game focuses on interface-driven investigation, editorial pressure, moral compromise, and dark humor inside a retro-cyberpunk world.

## Gameplay

The core gameplay is based on working through cases.

Each case usually includes:

1. Reading documents and reports
2. Finding the contradiction
3. Making an editorial decision
4. Managing the consequences
5. Protecting the newsroom and field reporters when possible

The player does not simply choose the “correct” answer every time. Some choices are safer, some are more honest, and some are very likely to make powerful people upset.

That is usually where the interesting part begins.

---

## Core Systems

### Document Investigation

The player reads official statements, field notes, logs, audio transcripts, and other documents to identify contradictions.

Official reports are calm, clean, and confident.
This is rarely a good sign.

### Headline Decisions

After investigating a case, the player chooses how the story should be published.

A headline can be:

* Safe
* Balanced
* Aggressive

Each option affects the newsroom and the story differently.

### Visual Evidence

Some cases require the player to inspect images or camera feeds to find hidden evidence.

### Nox Guidance Minigame

In some cases, the field reporter Nox needs help navigating dangerous areas through limited camera access.

The player guides Nox across a small tactical map while avoiding dangerous zones and blocked paths.

### Signal Routing Minigame

Some messages cannot travel through the official network.

The player routes unstable information through a backchannel system using different nodes, balancing signal quality and trace risk.

Fast routes may deliver cleaner data.
Safe routes may keep people alive.

Unfortunately, both are useful.

---

## Core Stats

Player choices affect several variables:

* **Public Trust**
* **Danger**
* **Money**
* **Reporter Safety**

These stats represent how the newsroom is doing and how dangerous the situation is becoming.

Publishing the truth can increase public trust, but it can also increase danger.
Playing it safe can protect the team, but it may slowly turn the newsroom into furniture.

## Characters

### Mara

Mara is the editor guiding the player through the night shift.

She is experienced, tired, sarcastic, and usually one official statement away from losing patience.

### Nox

Nox is a field reporter who keeps sending unstable messages from places he probably should not have entered.

He is useful.
This is not always the same thing as safe.

---

## World and Tone

**Nothing To Report** takes place in a corrupted future city where official systems are clean, polished, and suspiciously confident.

The tone combines:

* Cyber-noir atmosphere
* Retro terminal interfaces
* Dark humor
* Investigative journalism
* Bureaucratic absurdity
* Underground communication systems

The game is not meant to be a purely serious dystopia.
It is also about the strange comedy of systems that lie badly but with excellent formatting.
